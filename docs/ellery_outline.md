If I had $5 million — based off what I know now…

This is what chicago.com would look like. It would be a scroll-focused experience with user accounts that are both portable via ATProto and integrate with the not-quite-yet-outlined Chicago Public Media data pipeline.

A feed of neighborhood news. Based off Agate and the ranking of stories on the Sun-Times and WBEZ homepages, users will see stories that are relevant to their selected community area, to the area that they are currently (if location services are enabled) and to the city at large.

Things to do around you. Your feed also includes things like restaurants, upcoming events, murals and things to do in your selected community area or around you. This content is based off of coverage in the Sun-Times and WBEZ, user submitted reviews and staff curation.

Guide creation. Staff can create guides for neighorhoods or areas using relevant news articles, events, murals and other content that appears in the chicago.com feed. Users can create up to three private guides on their own that are attached to their chicago.com account.

Paywalled social features. Users can follow curators, creators and fellow public users (and ATProto integration would allow them to automatically follow people they follow on Bluesky), but they can’t create posts or public guides unless they become paid members of chicago.com.

Staff creators and curators. Curation, moderation and staff-produced guide creation is led by a small team including an event listing coordinator and 2-3 creator/influencers. They create guides, vertical videos and occasionally stories while interacting with users on the platform.
